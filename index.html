Protect Australians from Unsafe E-Bikes

Join the campaign to reverse the 2021 exemption that allowed unsafe imports.

What Changed in 2021?

In 2021, the Federal Government removed e-bikes from the Motor Vehicle Standards Act. This allowed imports without international safety compliance, leading to unsafe, overpowered e-bikes flooding the market.

Why It Matters

  • Unsafe imports with poor battery design
  • Crashes and fatalities involving non-compliant machines
  • Battery fires linked to low-quality imports
  • Youth riders using motorcycle-style e-bikes at dangerous speeds

Template Letter to Your MP

Copy and paste this letter to send to your local federal member:

Dear [Member’s Name],

I am writing to express my concern about the 2021 amendment to the Motor Vehicle Standards Act, which removed e-bikes from the definition of road vehicles. Since this change, unsafe imports have flooded the market, leading to crashes, injuries, and battery fires.

Advocacy groups such as Pedal Power ACT and We Ride Australia have documented how the removal of international safety standards eliminated protections like anti-tampering clauses. Without these safeguards, high-powered e-bikes capable of speeds well beyond 25 km/h are being sold illegally.

I urge you to support reinstating e-bikes under the Motor Vehicle Standards Act, ensuring all imports meet internationally recognised safety standards.

Yours sincerely,
[Your Name]
